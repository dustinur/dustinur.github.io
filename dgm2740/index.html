Metal Gear Solid V: The Phantom Pain 10/10

The Phantom Pain raises the bar for player agency through gameplay, never before had the words "play how you want" carried so much weight in any game I have ever played in the past. Go through like a ninja and sneak around to the objective, try sneaking around and if caught pull out your rifle and defend yourself against disgruntled guards, roll up guns blazing in a tank and destroy everything in sight, etc., etc. While these options are great, they come in to play the most if you find yourself dying a couple times at the same point. In past Metal Gear Solid games it just meant learning the AI's patrol paths and/or finding a different way around. These elements are still in place, however, the systems are so much more deep than anything I have experienced before. Keep getting caught? Try planting C4 on the enemy communications to detonate when you are spotted so it is impossible for them to call for backup. Find yourself at odds with a tank? Run to safety and call in an airstrike from your support chopper. Don't have the right equipment? Bang out some Side Ops and recruit more soldiers to level up the R&D team to produce better weapons and items. The game never fails to keep you engaged and busy doing meaningful things in either of the open world areas.
